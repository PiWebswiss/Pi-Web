AI Model to classify fossils

Identifying fossils can be a time-consuming task that relies on expert knowledge of fossil morphology. This task is particularly challenging due to the often fragmented and degraded nature of fossils.

The goal of the model is to provide a system that can accurately identify, classify, and recognize fossils based on their images.

These models are part of my Capstone Project, which you can find on GitHub.Capstone-Project

These models were trained on 8,000 images for the fossil vs non-fossil distinction and on 80,000 images across 46 different categories for the fossil classifier. The first model can determine if an image is a fossil or non-fossil with 93% accuracy, while the second has a 90% accuracy rate in identifying specific types of fossils.

It is important to note that these models are not 100% accurate and have their limitations. For example, the fossil classifier was trained only on 46 different categories, meaning it can classify images only within those predefined categories.

You can try the model below. The model is loaded and run on your computer.

Please Note:

The first time you use this application for inference, it may take a while. This is because the pre-trained models MobileNetV3Small and InceptionV3 are being loaded on your device.

I've chosen these models for their accuracy and efficiency, but their size means they require some time to become operational.

This loading process is a one-time event subsequent uses will be much faster. Thank you for your understanding.
